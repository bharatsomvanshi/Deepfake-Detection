What is Deep Fake ?
A deepfake is a type of synthetic media created using artificial intelligence, specifically deep learning techniques like Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs). Deepfakes are manipulated videos, images, or audio that convincingly depict people doing or saying things they never did. They can be used for entertainment, education, or satire, but they also pose risks such as misinformation, identity theft, and reputational damage. Deepfakes have raised concerns about their potential to spread false information, influence public opinion, or commit fraud. Efforts to detect deepfakes and address their misuse are underway, emphasizing the need for robust media literacy and legal frameworks.
